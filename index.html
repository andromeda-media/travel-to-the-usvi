Travel to the US Virgin Islands

The United States Virgin Islands is considered a jem of America with crystal clear waters and beautiful sandy beaches. There are three dazzling islands: St. Thomas, St. John, and St. Croix

Since the USVI is a US territory, you don't need to travel with passport from the US or Puerto Rico to the islands.

St. Thomas

St. Thomas holds the Virgin Islands' capital of Charlotte Amalie and is nicknamed "Rock City". This island is the most densely populated and is in close proximity to St. John, water island, and the small Hassle Island.

Book your trip today!

Most flights go to the Cyril E. King Airport on St. Thomas or the Henry E. Rohlsen Airport in St. Croix. If you are planning to visit one island but are flying in to one of the airports on another island, you will have to take a ferry or a sea plane to the island of your choice. For instance, many people fly in to the airport on St. Thomas and then take a ferry to St. John. Linked below you can find how to book tickets on a seaplane or ferry.

*It is easier to fly into the St. Thomas Cyril E. King Airport if you would like to visit St. John or Water Island because St. Thomas is closer to these two islands than St. Croix is.

    Ten Fun Facts About the USVI!

    1. Most of the island of St. John is National Park land
    2. The original name for Charlotte Amalie Harbor is "La Cerradura" which means "the lock"
    3. Many pirates have come through the Virgin Islands
    4. The original name for the town area was "Taphus" which meant "Tap on" in Danish for the free flowing rum
    5. The Virgin Islands was owned by the Danish until the U.S. bought it in 1917
    6. The Virgin Islands is the only place in the U.S. where you drive on the left side
    7. Camille Pizzaro is born in the USVI
    8. The legal drinking age is 18
    9. Point Udall in St. Croix is the Easternmost point in the U.S.
    10. U.S. founding father, Alexander Hamilton, grew up on St. Croix
